08 · Limitations & Open Problems

What the corpus flags about itself

The papers are, to their credit, candid about their own weaknesses. Grouped into families below, those caveats matter as much as the headline results — and the review itself is a search-shaped sample, which is the first limitation to state.

1 · Evaluation is Python-centric and benchmark-bound

◇ The same caveat, repeated across the corpus

Most empirical work is Python-only and tied to the SWE-bench family: the instruction-file benchmark Gloaguen+ '26, line-level pruning SWE-Pruner '26, perturbation probing RepoMirage '26, and memory-guided documentation MemDocAgent '26 all flag Python-only scope. Python is heavily represented in pretraining, so a model's parametric knowledge may mask (or inflate) the value of supplied context — niche languages and toolchains are untested.

Two further benchmark limits: SWE-Explore only contains instances some agent could already solve, so it "does not represent the full distribution of unsolved repo issues" SWE-Explore '26; and FastContext notes its benchmarks may overlap frontier-model pretraining, making results "controlled benchmark evidence rather than deployment guarantees" FastContext '26 Empirical.

2 · Many results rest on weak evidence designs

Several of the most architecturally interesting papers are explicitly not effect-size studies:

  • Observational, no causal claim. The codified-context infrastructure is one developer, one project: "No causal relationships are claimed; confounding factors including developer experience growth cannot be isolated" Vasilopoulos '26.
  • Self-study with usage estimates. "We do not claim a causal productivity improvement from this self-study"; token figures are ranges, not benchmarks PROJECTMEM '26.
  • Demo without baselines. One quantitative number (99/125) and no comparison against existing skill repositories SkillWiki '26.
  • Single sample, no variance. One run per agent and no significance tests on the instruction-file deltas Gloaguen+ '26; one ICAE architecture for the compression result Gelvan+ '26.

3 · Domain transfer and label-free signals

⚠ The governance numbers are not from coding

The corpus's most dramatic governance result (valid success 12%→96%) is from adversarial buyer–seller negotiation, not software engineering Shi+ '26; the other governance source is policy analysis of a public record through 7 May 2026, not a deployed system Manita & Amari '26. Both also rely on LLM-synthesized adversaries / a small incident set. Treat the execution-boundary pattern as promising and the coding-specific effect as unmeasured.

Two label-free methods also depend on the agent judging itself: harness self-optimization trusts past trajectories that "can carry adversarial content injected mid-task," risking entrenchment of bad behavior Pan+ '26; and the context map's value "depends on how the agent interacts with the context" — if interaction reveals little reusable knowledge, the map is worthless Peek '26 Empirical.

5 · Limits of this review

⟡ The honest scope of this site

This is a reading of 17 preprints, surfaced by an automated arXiv-relevance search for "context engineering for LLM coding agents" and gathered in June 2026. It is a sample shaped by the query, not a census: different search terms would surface a different 17. Two themes the request named are under-represented here and I said so up front — MCP appears in the wider pool mainly as security studies rather than context-provisioning work, and spec-driven / single-source-of-truth is covered by this hub's separate Spec-Driven Development review (see the hub) rather than duplicated here. Every paper dates to Jan–Jun 2026, so there is no longitudinal view and any "trend" reading would be my inference, not the corpus's. And these are mostly un-peer-reviewed preprints; the Evidence appraisal is deliberately conservative.

◇ The cross-cutting open problem

The corpus argues, in one breath, to codify more context as durable infrastructure and that context files, compression, and accumulation frequently make agents worse. What reconciles the two is maintenance — and no paper here solves it. Staleness is the named failure of codified context Vasilopoulos '26; fossilization is a measured smell dos Santos+ '26; maintainer review-capacity is the universally ungoverned governance dimension Manita & Amari '26; and self-maintaining harnesses risk silent regression Ning+ '26 Pan+ '26. Who keeps all this codified context correct, and how, at machine speed is the question the corpus circles but does not answer.
