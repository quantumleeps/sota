⟡ Appraisal — where the evidence is consistent vs thin

Most consistent. Three independent measurements agree that repository reading dominates an agent's token budget and that context quality tracks success SWE-Pruner '26 FastContext '26 SWE-Explore '26 — this is the corpus's firmest ground. The skeptical instruction-file results also reinforce each other across two independent groups Gloaguen+ '26 dos Santos+ '26.

Thinnest. The persistent-memory, codified-infrastructure, and governance claims rest on single-developer self-studies, demos without baselines, or a non-coding domain Vasilopoulos '26 PROJECTMEM '26 SkillWiki '26 Shi+ '26. They are good mechanism demonstrations, not effect sizes. And several headline benchmark wins carry an unquantified contamination risk, since 2026 frontier models may have seen the test repos FastContext '26.

◇ What would raise confidence (drawn from the papers' own future work)

Controlled, ablated benchmarking of each context tier with vs without it Vasilopoulos '26; multi-language evaluation beyond Python RepoMirage '26 SWE-Pruner '26; a "failures-prevented-per-N-commits" benchmark for memory gates PROJECTMEM '26; deployment of governance layers in actual coding (not negotiation) settings Shi+ '26; and variance/significance reporting for the instruction-file effects Gloaguen+ '26.
