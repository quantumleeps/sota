The measured-effect ledger

Every quantified result I pulled from these ten papers, with what was measured, the method and scale behind it, and my own confidence appraisal. The point of the ledger is to keep a number's weight visible: most rows come from a single benchmark, a single model family, or a single run, and the confidence column says so.

⟡ The "my confidence" column is mine, not the papers'

A coarse appraisal of how much I'd lean on each result, given its design — not a number from the source. Scale: Strong = controlled, significance-tested, or large-scale production; Moderate = clean measurement but single-setting / single-model / no significance tests; Indicative = synthetic data, single run, LLM-as-judge, or otherwise one-datapoint. All ten papers are recent, mostly un-peer-reviewed preprints; treat the whole table as provisional.

⟡ Appraisal — where the evidence is consistent vs thin

Most consistent across the corpus: the long-horizon ceiling. Independent of subfield, every paper that measured a multi-step trajectory found degradation — 26.3% workflow success Zou+ '26, ≤25% automata success with rising non-informative queries Menaged+ '26, escalation under transient failure Yang+ '26, retrieval decay over 10 weeks Chen+ '26. Different methods, same wall — that convergence is the table's most trustworthy signal.

Strongest single results: the controlled RAG ablation Shaikh '26, the runtime-detection recall lift Zhang+ '26, and the production serving wins Huang+ '26 — each has either significance testing or large-scale deployment behind it.

Thinnest: anything on synthetic data with an LLM judge (AdaMem) or single-run / synthetic-underspecification (clarification F1), and the ρ=−0.13 figure, which is real but n=13 and cited rather than re-run. Believe the direction of these; don't quote the magnitude as settled.

◇ What would raise confidence (from the papers' own future work)

Larger, non-synthetic memory cohorts over year-long horizons and without LLM-as-judge Chen+ '26; the pre-registered predictive-validity pilot (n=80 paired ranks, 1,200 perturbed observations) the eval paper proposes but hasn't run Patel+ '26; matched negative controls and multi-rater precision audits for runtime scanning Zhang+ '26; sandboxed executable tools and longer-than-five-turn trajectories for privilege selection Yang+ '26; and moving the uncertainty decomposition from the prompt into the model via alignment training to test whether the gains survive once capability dilution is removed Matsnev '26.
